The Sacred Fire

"This world, which is the same for all, no one of gods or men has made; but it was ever, is now, and ever shall be an ever-living fire, with measures of it kindling and measures going out."
Heraclitus · Fragment 30 · c. 500 BCE

There is a fire at the center of a fully lived life. Not metaphor — the actual animating force that makes the difference between a life that works and a life that burns.

Most of us have touched it. The moment of genuine clarity that arrived without being reasoned toward. The encounter with another person that changed something permanently. The knowing that preceded the evidence. We filed these moments under coincidence, or luck, or just one of those things — and moved on.

Hieron Pyr is a book about what those moments actually are, why they arrive when they do, and how to build a life in which they arrive more often — and matter more when they do.
